About

The primary intention of this web application is to micrograph nematodes in the West Kootenay area and to facilitate identifying unidentified nematodes through micrographs. The user can select nematode characteristics (bodies, heads, tails, esophagi, amphids, vulvas, reproductive tracts, etc.) in micrographs of identified nematodes and compare those features to a nematode specimen under investigation. Then, the user decides the identity of the nematode based on similarities.

The nematodes on the UNL pages of web application reference identified nematodes found on the University of Nebraska Nematology's Lab website to facilitate visual identification of nematodes to one's ability and skill at nematode taxonomy. Nematodes are a cryptic species. Scientists know the most about a specific species, Caenorhabditis elegans, and, at the same time, the least about the majority of other species. Therefore, one needs to recognize, identify and measure complex multiple morphological characters to determine family, genus or species. In addition, the observer examines numerous similar nematodes. Some nematodes mounted for viewing render well, and some do not. Therefore, one keeps preparing slides, looking at, measuring, consulting experts and thumbing through papers of previously identified nematodes to find one that matches and reveals the identity of the nematode under investigation.
