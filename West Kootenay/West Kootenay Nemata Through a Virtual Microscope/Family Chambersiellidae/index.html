Keys for Class Chromadorea to Sub-class Rhabditia to Family Chambersiellidae

  1. pore-like or slit-like amphid apertures, always labial.
  2. deirids present in some near nerve ring.
  3. phasmids present, generally posterior.
  4. no hypodermal glands, but hypodermal cells are multinucleate.
  5. excretory system tubular.
  6. no caudal glands.
  7. male generally with a single testis.
  8. caudal alae common.
  9. male with paired preanal supplement glands in two sublateral rows.
  10. sensory papillae cephalic only, although may be caudal papillae in males.
  11. esophagus varies but has 3 esophageal glands.
  12. almost exclusively terrestrial, rarely freshwater or marine.
  13. Amphid apertures on lips, often difficult to see; no hypodermal glands; hypodermal cells are multinucleate; no caudal glands; male with a single testis and with paired pre-anal supplements, subventral in 2 rows; 3 esophageal glands
  14. Three-part esophagus (corpus, isthmus and postcorpus), valve in postcorpus; corpus and postcorpus in 12 cells
  15. three-part esophagus (corpus, isthmus and postcorpus),
  16. valve in postcorpus
  17. corpus and postcorpus comprise 12 cells
  18. Cuticle finely striated with two lateral incisures.
  19. Lip region with six cephalic cirri (elaborate cephalic appedages) and 6-10 cephalic papillae.
  20. Anteriormost rhabdions of the stoma modified to form hooklike structures and distinct from the rest.
  21. Posterior part of stoma forming a narrow vaselike glottid apparatus and surrounded by tissue which is differentiated from that of the esophagus.
  22. Amphids opening behind the anterior broad chamber of the stoma;.
  23. Esophagus without a median bulb but with a valvulated terminal bulb.
  24. Monovarial.
  25. Vulva slightly pre-equatorial to postequatorial.
  26. Testis single.
  27. Male tail with several pairs of caudal papillae;
  28. Spicules paired and not joined; gubernaculum present;
  29. Rectal glands present;
  30. Tail of both sexes with a dorsally hooked terminus.
