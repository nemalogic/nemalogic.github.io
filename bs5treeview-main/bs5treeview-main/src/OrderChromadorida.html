The Chromadorida are an order of nematodes. Nematodes are unsegmented roundworms with elongated, bilaterally symmetrical bodies. They are microscopic invertebrates found in almost every habitat on Earth, including marine, freshwater, and terrestrial environments.

Chromadorida nematodes are typically characterized by the following morphological features:

  • Body: Cylindrical and elongated, with a tapering head and tail.
  • Cuticle: The outer covering of the body, which may be smooth, striated (with grooves), or ornamented (with bumps or ridges).
  • Head: Contains a mouth, sensory organs (amphids and cephalic setae), and sometimes eyes (ocelli).
  • Mouth: Surrounded by a buccal cavity, which may have teeth or other structures for feeding.
  • Pharynx: A muscular organ that pumps food into the intestine.
  • Intestine: A long, tube-shaped organ that absorbs nutrients from food.
  • Reproductive system: Separate sexes, with males having a single testis and females having two ovaries.
  • Tail: Can be short and blunt or long and slender.
