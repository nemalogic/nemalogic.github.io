The suborder Rhabditina within nematodes exhibits a specific morphology characterized by several key features:

  • Body: Elongated and cylindrical, bilaterally symmetrical, with a tapered anterior (head) end and a more blunt posterior (tail) end.
  • Cuticle: A tough, non-cellular outer layer protecting the body.
  • Musculature: Longitudinal muscles running along the body for locomotion.
  • Digestive System: Complete digestive system with a mouth, muscular pharynx for food processing, a straight intestine, and an anus.
  • Sensory Organs: Amphids (sensory organs near the head) for chemoreception and phasmids (sensory organs near the tail) for mechanosensation in some species.
  • Excretory System: Rete tubules, a network of canals that collect waste products and expel them through excretory pores.
  • Reproductive System: Separate sexes, with males having a single testis and females having paired ovaries.

Additional characteristics of Rhabditina may include:

  • Buccal cavity: A specialized mouth cavity for feeding, sometimes with teeth or other structures for scraping or piercing.
  • Bursas: Copulatory structures in males, often paired, located near the tail.
  • Spicules: Chitinous structures used for copulation in males.
  • Vulva: The female reproductive opening on the ventral side of the body.

Variations within Rhabditina:

  • Some species may have a reduced or absent pharyngeal apparatus if they have a symbiotic lifestyle.
  • The number and arrangement of body pores can vary among species.
  • The presence and structure of specialized reproductive organs like spicules and bursas can differ.
