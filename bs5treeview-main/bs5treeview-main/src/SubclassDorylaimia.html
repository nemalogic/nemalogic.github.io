Dorylaimida, commonly referred to as Dorylaims, are a large order of nematodes (roundworms). Their morphology is quite distinct, with some key features setting them apart from other nematodes. Here's a breakdown of their key morphological characteristics:

  • Odontostyle: One of the most defining features of Dorylaimida is the presence of a movable mural tooth or a hollow spear called the odontostyle. This structure originates from a migrated stomal cell, the cheilostome, and is used for feeding. The cheilostome itself can be further divided into a vestibulum and a guiding apparatus, which may appear as a single or double ring around the odontostyle .
  • Body Wall: Dorylaims typically have a well-developed body wall consisting of four layers: the cuticle, epidermis, body-cavity muscles, and hypodermis. The cuticle, the outermost layer, provides protection and support. The epidermis secretes the cuticle and plays a role in nutrient uptake and waste removal. The body-cavity muscles allow for locomotion, while the hypodermis helps maintain body shape and contributes to molting.
  • Digestive System: The digestive system of Dorylaimida is relatively simple. The mouth opening leads to a muscular pharynx that pumps food into the intestine. The intestine is a long, tubular structure where food is digested and absorbed. Waste products are eliminated through the anus located at the posterior end of the body.
  • Reproductive System: Dorylaims are dioecious, meaning there are separate sexes. Males have a single testis that produces sperm, while females have two ovaries that produce eggs. Fertilization is internal, and females lay eggs that develop into juveniles.
  • Nervous System: The nervous system of Dorylaimida is relatively simple, consisting of a nerve ring located around the esophagus and several longitudinal nerves that run along the body. The nerve ring coordinates sensory input and motor output.

These are just some of the key morphological features of Dorylaimida. Their specific morphology can vary depending on the species, but these general characteristics provide a good overview of what makes them unique within the nematode phylum.
