The Desmoscolecida are an order of nematodes characterized by a unique body structure different from other nematode groups. Here's a breakdown of their morphology:

  • Body Wall
    • Thick with four layers: cuticle, epidermis, basement membrane, and musculature.
    • The cuticle is further divided into three layers: the outer layer (corneum), the middle layer (mediam), and the inner layer (basale).
    • The presence of a deirid (sensory organ) close to the head distinguishes them from other nematodes .
  • Head
    • Distinct, set off from the body by a neck constriction.
    • Contains a mouth surrounded by six lips.
    • Four cephalic setae (sensory bristles) are present on the head.
  • Amphimictic reproduction
    • Desmoscolecids reproduce sexually with separate sexes (male and female).
  • Digestive System
    • A muscular pharynx for feeding.
    • A straight intestine ending in an anus located ventrally near the tail.
  • Nervous System
    • Consists of a nerve ring surrounding the esophagus and longitudinal nerve cords.
  • Excretory System
    • Includes a pair of renette cells that eliminate liquid waste products.
  • Reproductive System
    • Males have a single testis and a spicule for sperm transfer.
    • Females have paired ovaries and oviducts with a vulva located on the ventral side.
  • Tail
    • Short and conical, with a single terminal sensory papilla.

Here are some additional points to note about Desmoscolecida morphology:

  • They are generally small, measuring from 0.3 to 5.0 millimeters in length.
  • Their body shape is cylindrical and elongated.
  • Most Desmoscolecida are free-living, found in marine and brackish water sediments.
  • Some species are parasitic on other invertebrates.
